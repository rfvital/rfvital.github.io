RF-Vital: Radio-Based Contactless Respiration Monitoring for a Moving Individual

Jae-Ho Choi, Ki-Bong Kang, Kyung-Tae Kim,
Stanford University, Samsung Electronics, POSTECH
Code Data

Abstract

The non-contact respiration rate measurement (nRRM) method enables a system to monitor the breathing patterns of an individual without physical contact, which is essential for consistent health monitoring in everyday life. Current nRRM approaches heavily rely on capturing subtle variations in RGB profiles reflected from a camera to extract respiration signals remotely. Their requirement for continuous pixel-level tracking, however, poses significant practical constraints, limiting their applicability to individuals in quasi-stationary sitting positions. To overcome this limitation, we propose a radio-frequency (RF)-Vital model, which leverages RF signals to widen the applicability of nRRM approaches to non-stationary individuals who exhibit global motions (GMs) and even walk around. The core idea of the RF-Vital model lies in the unique characteristics of RF signals: the RF signals received from a moving individual capture both their respiratory motions (RMs) and GMs through linear superposition, while simultaneously providing the reflections of GM alone. To fully utilize such unique properties, we introduce a new RF modality that allows stable involvement of micro-level respiration signatures, even in the presence of GMs. Furthermore, we optimize the RF-Vital model using a novel multi-task adversarial learning framework combined with a new loss function, which facilitates the direct mapping of the desired RMs as well as the self-supervised removal of GMs, thereby effectively filtering out RMs from mixtures of GMs and RMs. The proposed RF-vital model was evaluated through newly published datasets, demonstrating a state-of-the-art performance in static conditions as well as achieving the significant milestone of achieving nRRM under moving conditions.
